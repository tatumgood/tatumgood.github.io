SIGCSE Response Paper

Theses

  1. The SIGCSE community needs to have a serious conversation about the inherent biases and shortcomings in its peer-review process.
  2. How can we ensure that peer reviews in SIGCSE and similar academic communities are more inclusive and considerate of diverse perspectives?
  3. What steps can be taken to address the dominance of white individuals and elitism within SIGCSE, particularly in decision-making processes?
  4. Is there a need for specialized training or guidelines for reviewers in SIGCSE to better evaluate proposals related to diversity, equity, and inclusion in computing education?
  5. Should SIGCSE consider implementing anonymous or blind reviews to mitigate biases based on authors' identities or affiliations?
  6. What role should SIGCSE leadership play in promoting a more inclusive and equitable environment for all members of the community?
  7. Are there systemic barriers within SIGCSE that prevent meaningful progress toward diversity, equity, and inclusion?
  8. How can we ensure that feedback provided during the peer-review process is constructive and helpful for authors, regardless of their backgrounds?
  9. Should SIGCSE establish a dedicated committee or task force to address DEI-related concerns and develop strategies for improvement?
  10. What impact do biased reviews and decisions have on the overall integrity and reputation of conferences like SIGCSE?
  11. How can SIGCSE encourage more participation and contributions from underrepresented groups in computing education?
  12. Are there examples of best practices from other academic communities that SIGCSE could adopt to promote fairness and inclusivity in its processes?
  13. What accountability measures should be in place to address instances of discriminatory or biased behavior within SIGCSE?
  14. How can SIGCSE ensure that its policies, practices, and culture align with its stated goals of fostering diversity, equity, and inclusion in CS education?
  15. How can SIGCSE foster a culture of constructive criticism and continuous improvement while also addressing systemic inequalities in academia?
